# Cycle 10 — Mid-cycle "working on / what surprised me" post

> Draft for public posting (LinkedIn narrative). Review before publishing.

---

**This cycle is the EU AI Act deployer readout — and step one was throwing out my
own notes and re-checking the law, because it had already moved.**

When I drafted the plan months ago, I wrote down the headline dates and the status
of the "Digital Omnibus" (the EU's package that would delay parts of the AI Act).
Rule one of governance content: **never publish a date you haven't re-verified at
build time.** So I did. Two things had shifted:

1. The **Digital Omnibus** is now at **provisional agreement** (reached 7 May 2026)
   — but it is **not yet formally adopted and not in the Official Journal.** That
   matters enormously: its deferrals are **proposed, not law.** Anyone telling a
   client "high-risk obligations are pushed to December 2027, relax" is quoting a
   deadline that does not legally exist yet. The honest instruction is: **plan
   against 2 August 2026** and treat the delay as likely-but-not-guaranteed.
2. A **Code of Practice on AI-generated-content transparency** was published 10 June
   2026 — a concrete route to demonstrate Article 50 compliance that didn't exist
   when I wrote the plan.

The other trap I'm being careful to avoid: **conflating the penalty tiers.** You
see "€35M or 7%" quoted for everything. That cap is **only** for Article 5
prohibited practices. Deployer obligations are **€15M or 3%**; bad information to
regulators is **€7.5M or 1%.** Getting this wrong is an instant credibility tell.

The framing I'm landing on for mid-market: **most of your agents are limited-risk,
not high-risk.** A support-triage agent isn't on the Annex III list. So your real
near-term homework is small — transparency, AI literacy, and not deploying a
prohibited use — and the heavy machinery only switches on if you're in a high-risk
domain. That's the readout I wish someone had handed me.
